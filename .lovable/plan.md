

# SEO Audit and Long-Tail Keyword Strategy -- CRUMS Leasing

## Part 1: What the Data Tells Us (Why You Rank the Way You Do)

### Growth Story
Your site went from **8 impressions/day** in late November to **1,300+ impressions/day** by mid-February -- a 160x increase in under 3 months. This is a textbook new-site crawl ramp: Google discovered your pages in waves, starting with branded queries, then informational content, then location pages.

```text
Late Nov:     ~5 imp/day    (branded only)
Early Dec:    ~40 imp/day   (Google indexing tools/guides)
Early Jan:    ~500 imp/day  (dry van dimensions explode)
Mid-Feb:      ~1,000+ imp/day (location + long-tail kicking in)
```

### Where Your Traffic Actually Comes From

| Category | Impressions | Clicks | What's Happening |
|----------|------------|--------|------------------|
| Branded ("crums leasing") | 360 | 116 | Healthy. 32% CTR. |
| Dry van dimensions/specs | ~15,000+ | ~13 | Massive impressions, almost zero clicks. Position 5-10 for dozens of "53 dry van dimensions" variants. |
| Trucking calculators (per diem, fuel, CPM) | ~6,000+ | ~30 | Tools drive discovery but low CTR. Position 13-54. |
| Trailer leasing keywords | ~1,200+ | ~3 | Position 22-71. Barely visible. This is the problem. |
| Location queries (Atlanta, Austin, Chicago) | ~3,500+ | ~17 | Position 15-75. Showing up but not ranking well. |
| Flatbed specs/dimensions | ~2,500 | ~4 | Similar to dry van -- informational, not converting. |

### The Core Problem

**You rank for INFORMATIONAL queries (dimensions, calculators) but NOT for COMMERCIAL queries (leasing, rental, lease).** The site acts as a trucking encyclopedia, not a leasing company in Google's eyes.

Key evidence:
- "dry van trailer leasing" -- 358 impressions, position **34** (page 3-4)
- "trailer leasing" -- 49 impressions, position **69** (page 7)
- "semi trailer leasing" -- 61 impressions, position **71**
- "commercial trailer leasing" -- 26 impressions, position **41**
- "flatbed trailer leasing" -- 15 impressions, position **23**

Meanwhile:
- "53 dry van dimensions" -- 143 impressions, position **5**
- "dry van trailer height" -- 27 impressions, position **1**
- "dry van interior dimensions" -- 55 impressions, position **3**

### The /dry-van-trailers Page Problem

This is your biggest single opportunity. It has **15,655 impressions** but only **13 clicks (0.08% CTR)** at position 7.29. A page at position 7 should have 3-5% CTR minimum. The likely causes:

1. The page title/meta description is optimized for "leasing" but the queries triggering it are about **dimensions and specifications**
2. Users searching "53 dry van dimensions" see your page but the snippet doesn't answer their question, so they skip it
3. The page tries to do two things (sell leasing AND answer dimension questions) and does neither perfectly in the SERP

### Reefer Redirect Is Losing Traffic

`/refrigerated-trailers` redirects to `/services/trailer-leasing` but you have **683 impressions** on the old URL and queries like:
- "reefer trailer leasing" -- 30 imp, pos 16.6
- "refrigerated trailer lease" -- 30 imp, pos 25
- "lease refrigerated trailer" -- 24 imp, pos 24

Since you don't offer reefer trailers, these impressions will naturally fade, but the redirect is correct.

## Part 2: Strategy to Increase Impressions and Clicks

### Priority 1: Fix the /dry-van-trailers CTR Crisis (Biggest Quick Win)

The page gets 15,655 impressions at position 7 but 0.08% CTR. Improving CTR to even 2% = **300+ clicks/month** from the same rankings.

**Actions:**
- Add a prominent **"53' Dry Van Trailer Dimensions & Specs"** section near the top of `/dry-van-trailers` with a clearly formatted specs table (length, width, height, cubic feet, weight capacity) -- this is what 80%+ of the queries are actually asking for
- Update the meta title to: `53' Dry Van Trailer Dimensions & Leasing | CRUMS` (currently it's leasing-focused but the queries are spec-focused)
- Update the meta description to lead with dimensions: `53' dry van interior dimensions: 53'L x 8'6"W x 9'H (3,489 cu ft). Lease dry van trailers from CRUMS - flexible terms, no credit check.`
- Add FAQ schema with the top dimension questions ("What are the interior dimensions of a 53' dry van?", "How many cubic feet in a 53 foot trailer?")

### Priority 2: Create Dedicated Leasing Landing Pages (Attack Commercial Intent)

Your money keywords ("dry van trailer leasing", "trailer leasing", "flatbed trailer leasing") are all position 30-70 because no page specifically targets them. The current `/services/trailer-leasing` page is too generic.

**New pages to create:**

1. **`/dry-van-trailer-leasing`** -- Dedicated page for "dry van leasing", "dry van trailer lease", "lease dry van", "dry van trailer leasing near me". Target: 500+ combined monthly impressions at position 30+ today.

2. **`/flatbed-trailer-leasing`** -- For "flatbed trailer leasing", "flatbed lease", "flatbed trailer lease". Currently position 22-60 for these terms.

3. **`/semi-trailer-leasing`** -- For "semi trailer leasing", "commercial trailer leasing", "trailer leasing companies". Currently position 40-83.

4. **`/trailer-leasing-texas`** (or `/locations/texas-trailer-leasing`) -- For "trailer leasing texas", "trailer leasing houston tx", "dry van trailer leasing fort worth tx". Multiple Texas-specific queries at position 15-40.

Each page should include:
- H1 matching the primary keyword exactly
- Pricing transparency (monthly rate ranges)
- Comparison table (lease vs buy vs rent)
- Internal links to the calculator tools and relevant guides
- Location-specific CTAs
- FAQs with long-tail keyword variations
- Structured data (Service schema)

### Priority 3: Strengthen Location Pages for Rental/Leasing Queries

Atlanta is your best-performing location (1,508 impressions) but at position 33.7. The queries show clear commercial intent:

- "dry van trailer rental atlanta ga" -- 36 imp, pos 8.5
- "semi trailer leasing atlanta" -- 29 imp, pos 11
- "trailer lease purchase atlanta" -- 31 imp, pos 11.3
- "flatbed trailer rental atlanta" -- 43 imp, pos 12.2

**Actions:**
- Add dedicated "Trailer Leasing in [City]" sections to each location page with specific trailer types, pricing, and availability
- Add FAQs to location pages targeting "[city] trailer rental/leasing" long-tails
- Strengthen internal linking: every mention of a city on the site should link to its location page
- Add LocalBusiness schema to each location page

### Priority 4: Optimize Calculator/Tool Pages for Conversion

Your calculators drive significant impressions:
- Per diem calculator: 3,191 impressions, 18 clicks
- Fuel calculator: 1,118 impressions, 5 clicks
- Cost per mile: 853 impressions, 2 clicks
- Tools hub: 1,762 impressions, 4 clicks

These users are truckers researching their business -- your exact target audience. But the pages aren't converting discovery into lease inquiries.

**Actions:**
- Add a persistent "Looking for a trailer?" CTA banner on every calculator page
- Add contextual internal links: "Calculate your cost per mile -- then see how leasing from CRUMS can lower it"
- Improve the `/resources/tools` hub page meta: currently position 42, should target "trucking calculators" (379 impressions) and "trucking cost per mile calculator" (169 impressions)

### Priority 5: Internal Linking Overhaul

Create a hub-and-spoke model centered on your money pages:

```text
/dry-van-trailer-leasing (NEW hub)
  |-- /dry-van-trailers (specs spoke)
  |-- /resources/guides/why-leasing-dry-van (guide spoke)
  |-- /resources/guides/choosing-trailer (guide spoke)
  |-- /resources/tools/lease-vs-buy (tool spoke)
  |-- /locations/atlanta-ga (location spoke)
  |-- /locations/chicago-il (location spoke)
  |-- /industries/owner-operators (audience spoke)
  |-- /industries/fleet-leasing (audience spoke)

/flatbed-trailer-leasing (NEW hub)
  |-- /flatbed-trailers (specs spoke)
  |-- /resources/guides/choosing-trailer (shared spoke)
  |-- /resources/tools/lease-vs-buy (shared spoke)
```

Every spoke page should link back to the hub with anchor text like "Lease a dry van trailer" or "Get a dry van trailer lease quote."
